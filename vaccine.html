Vaccination

What is vaccine?

COVID-19 vaccine helps in building up immunity of your body against the coronavirus that causes COVID-19. There are different type of vaccines available for now such as Phizer-BioNTech, AstraZeneca etc.

Learn more about vaccination

Herd Immunity

Vaccination helps to prevent the spreading of COVID-19 in the societies. When the number of individuals receiving vaccines increase, it forms a herd immunity that provide substantial protection to those who have not receive vaccines. Vaccination not only protecting yourself, but it also protects the others in the community who are not be able to receive vaccine. So, if you are able to, get vaccinated.

Type of Vaccines in Malaysia

There are currently 5 type of vaccines approved to be used in Malaysia

(Updated on 15th July 2021)

AstraZeneca

AstraZeneca, known as Oxford-AstraZeneca or AZ vaccine. It was developed by Oxford University and AstraZeneca, a British-Swedish company. It is a type of Adenovirus vaccine.


AstraZeneca vaccineclose
Type of Vaccine:
  • Viral Vector Type
Number of doses:
  • 2 doses (0.5ml each dose)
  • second dose is after 4-12 weeks of the first dose received
Suitable to:
  • Individuals 18 years and above
  • Individuals with comorbidities that has been identified as increasing the risk of severe COVID-19, including cardiovascular disease, respiratory disease, and obesity
  • Individuals who has COVID-19 in past
Not Suitable to:
  • Individuals with age below 18
  • History of anaphylaxis to previous non COVID-19 vaccines, injectable medicines of multiple different drug classes, or substances possibly containing polysorbate or PEG
  • Individuals with previous history of severe allergic reactions to vaccines after previous dose of AZ COVID-19 vaccine
Possible side effect after receiving AstraZeneca vaccine:
  • Common Symptom: Headache, Nausea, Myalgia, arthralgia, injection site tenderness, injection site pain, fatigue and chills
  • Uncommon Symptom: Lymphadenopathy, decreased appetite, dizziness, somnolence, hyperhidrosis, pruritus and rash

Janssen(Johnson & Johnson)

Janssen COVID-19 vaccine, as known as Johnson & Johnson COVID-19 vaccine. It was developed in Leiden, Netherlands by Janssen Vaccines together with Janssen Pharmaceuticals and Johnson & Johnson.


Janssen (Johnson & Johnson)close
Type of Vaccine:
  • Viral Vector Type
Number of doses:
  • 1 doses (0.5ml)
Suitable to:
  • Individuals 18 years and above
  • breastfeeding individuals
  • Individuals that has COVID-19 in past
Not Suitable to:
  • Pregnant women
  • Individuals with history of anaphylaxis
  • Individuals with body temperature more than 38.5 °C
  • Individuals age below 18
  • Individuals with previous history of severe allergic reactions to the vaccine after previous dose of Janssen COVID-19 vaccine
Possible side effect after receiving AstraZeneca vaccine:
  • Common Symptom: Injection site pain, headache, nausea, myalgia, fatigue, cough, arthralgia, pyrexia, chills.
  • Uncommon Symptom: Rash, hypersensitivity, thrombosis in combination with thrombocytopenia

Pfizer/BioNTech (Comirnaty®

Pfizer-BioNTech COVID-19 vaccine is developed by BioNTech company from German. This mRNA-based vaccine is has proven to have up to 95% effectiveness in preventing symptomatic disease for those without prior infection.


Pfizer/BioNTechclose
Type of Vaccine:
  • mRNA Type
Number of doses:
  • 2 doses (0.3ml)
  • Second dose is after 3-4 weeks of the first dose
Suitable to:
  • Individuals 18 years and above
  • breastfeeding individuals
  • Individuals that has COVID-19 in past
  • Individuals with various conditions that are associated with increased risk of severe disease such as diabetes, asthma, pulmonary, liver and kidney disease
Not Suitable to:
  • Individuals age below 18 years
  • Pregnant women
  • History of Anaphylaxis to injectable medicines of multiple different drug classes
  • Individuals with history of severe allergic reactions to the vaccine after previous dose
  • Acute febrile illness
Possible side effect after receiving AstraZeneca vaccine:
  • Common Symptom: Injection site pain, headache, nausea, myalgia, fatigue, cough, arthralgia, pyrexia, chills.
  • Uncommon Symptom: Insomnia, lymphadenopathy, malaise, extremity pain, Bell's Palsy, myocariditis

CanSinoBio(Convidecia)

The AD5-nCOV vaccine, with trade name Convidecia, is developed by CanSino Biologics. Its shown that this vaccine has an efficacy of 65.7% towards the moderate cases of COVID-19 and 90.98% efficacy at preventing severe COVID-19 cases.


CanSinoBio(Convidecia)close
Type of Vaccine:
  • Viral Vector Type
Number of doses:
  • 1 doses (0.5ml)
  • Second dose is after 3-4 weeks of the first dose
Suitable to:
  • Individuals 18 years and above
Not Suitable to:
  • Pregnant women and breastfeeding woman
  • History of Anaphylaxis to injectable medicines of multiple different drug classes
  • Individuals with history of severe allergic reactions to the vaccine after previous dose
  • Individuals with uncontrolled epilepsy and other progressive neurological disease
  • Individuals with Guillain-Barre Syndrome
Possible side effect after receiving AstraZeneca vaccine:
  • Common Symptom: Injection site pain, headache, nausea, myalgia, fatigue, drowsiness, diarrhoea, joint pain, cough, loss appetite
  • Uncommon Symptom: Injection site rash, bleeding, cellulitis

Sinovac (CoronaVac®)

Sinovac vaccine is developed by Sinovac Biotech. According to Malaysia's health ministry announcement, Malaysia will stop administering Sinovac vaccines once its supplies end.


Sinovacclose
Type of Vaccine:
  • Inactivated (Vero Cell)
Number of doses:
  • 2 doses (0.5ml)
  • Second dose is between 2-4 weeks after the first dose
Suitable to:
  • Individuals 18 years and above
  • Individuals with comorbidities that has been identified as increasing the risk of severe COVID-19, including cardiovascular disease, respiratory disease, and obesity
  • Individuals who has COVID-19 in the past.
  • breastfeeding woman
  • Individuals who are immunocompromised
Not Suitable to:
  • Individuals age below 18.
  • Individuals with history of anaphylaxis to any component of the vaccine
  • Individuals with acute PCR-confirmed COVID-19
  • Individuals with body temperature over 38.5°C
  • Individuals with uncontrolled severe chronic diseases
  • Individuals with severe neurological conditions such as Guillain-Barre syndrome
Possible side effect after receiving AstraZeneca vaccine:
  • Common Symptom: Injection site pain, injection site swelling, headache, nausea, fatigue, diarrhoea, joint pain, cough, shivering, itchiness, loss of appetitie, runny nose, stuffy nose, stomacache
  • Uncommon Symptom: Injection site burning sensation, vomitting, hypersensity, abnormal skin and mucous membrane condition, fever, trembling, flushing, swelling, dizziness, drowsiness, muscle cramp, nose bleeds, bloating, swelling of eyelids, constipation, diminished sense of smell, hiccups, eye redness

Information are obtained from WHO and Clinical Guidelines on COVID-19 Vaccination in Malaysia
